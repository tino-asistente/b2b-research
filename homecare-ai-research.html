Market Context

The US home care market includes ~11,500 Medicare-certified home health agencies and thousands more non-medical/private-duty agencies (est. 30,000+ total). The industry faces a severe caregiver shortage — all 50 states report workforce shortages. Nearly half of US states are at the threshold of a caregiving emergency (Otsuka 2024). Average caregiver wage is $16.82/hr (BLS 2024), barely above fast food ($15.07/hr).

Key pain points addressable by AI:

  • Shift filling: 274,950+ scheduling coordinator job postings on Indeed alone — massive manual labor
  • After-hours calls: Agencies spend $200-$2,200+/mo on answering services at $0.75-$1.50/min
  • Missed revenue: Agencies routinely turn away clients due to staffing gaps
  • Silver Tsunami: 4.1M Americans turned 65 in 2024 — demand accelerating

Product-Market Fit Summary

🤖 AI Staff Scheduler

  • Detects cancellations → auto-calls available caregivers
  • Replaces $42K-$49K/yr staffing coordinator salary
  • Addresses #1 pain: shift coverage gaps
  • Must integrate with AxisCare, WellSky, HHAeXchange
  • TCPA: Healthcare exemption for care-related calls to landlines; mobile requires prior consent

📞 AI Receptionist

  • 24/7 inbound call handling, intake, scheduling
  • Replaces $200-$2,200/mo answering services
  • Captures leads that currently go to voicemail
  • Competitors: Smith.ai ($97/mo), Dialzara ($29/mo), traditional answering services
  • Must be HIPAA-compliant for PHI handling

⚠️ US Regulatory Risks

  • HIPAA Compliance: AI Receptionist handling patient info must be HIPAA-compliant. Requires BAA with agencies, encrypted storage, audit logs. Violation fines: $100-$50,000 per incident.
  • TCPA (Telephone Consumer Protection Act): AI Staff Scheduler makes outbound auto-calls to caregivers.
    • Healthcare exemption allows prerecorded calls to residential landlines without consent (max 1/day, 3/week)
    • Mobile phones REQUIRE prior express consent for autodialed/prerecorded calls — most caregivers use mobile
    • Solution: Get written consent during caregiver onboarding. Include opt-in in employment agreement.
    • Penalties: $500-$1,500 per violation (willful)
  • State-Specific Regulations: Some states (CA, NY, IL) have stricter auto-call laws. California requires two-party consent for call recording.
  • FCC 2024 Update: Tightened ATDS definition. AI-generated voice may be classified as "artificial voice" requiring consent.
  • Mitigation: Build opt-in consent collection into onboarding flow. Offer SMS-first with voice as fallback. Maintain consent records.
